Industrial Research

Defect location in isotropic media for Non-destructive Ultrasonic Testing [2011]

General Electric, Global Research Center, John F. Welch Technology Center
  • Developed a ray tracing algorithm for longitudinal ultrasonic waves in isotropic homogeneous material, and modelled the same on the cross section of a circular pipe.
  • Developed a 3D Visualization tool in C++ and OpenGL to represent the amplitude and Time of Flight information onto the actual inspection geometry.
  • Combined data from several data channels to accurately determine the location of defects within the weld geometry.
  • Designed and conducted experiments to validate the accuracy of the simulation results.

Improvement of packing accuracy of volumetric filling machines [2012]

ITC Ltd.
  • Developed an Event-based PID controller using the open-source electronics platform, Arduino to provide real time feedback for a bulk-material packaging process.
  • Used Control Charts to identify and mitigate sources of variation by applying Western-Electric and Nelson rules.
  • Designed mechanical attachments to the Volumetric Feeder to improve accuracy by 50%, after a comprehensive study of the dynamics of the machine and bulk flow properties of the material being packaged.
